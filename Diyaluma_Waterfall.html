Diyaluma Ella

Description

The most beautiful waterfall in Sri Lanka is Diyaluma Falls. One of Sri Lanka's tallest waterfalls, Diyaluma Falls is also regarded as one of the most stunning. The falls are situated about 150 kilometers from Colombo's capital city in the Central Province's Knuckles Mountain Range. The water pours down two distinct tiers at the Diyaluma Falls since it is a double waterfall. The first tier rises to a height of 160 meters (525 feet), whereas the second tier is just 109 meters (358 feet) tall. The falls are the second-highest in Sri Lanka due to their overall height of 269 meters (883 ft). The Diyaluma River, which has its source in the Knuckles Mountain Range, feeds the Diyaluma Falls. Before rushing over the edge of the falls, the river travels through a narrow valley. Following that, the water cascades down the two tiers, providing an amazing spectacle. The monsoon season, which lasts from October to December, is when the Diyaluma Falls are at their most spectacular. The falls are at their height at this time, and the water is a vivid blue tint.

Legend

The Diyaluma Falls can be viewed in a number of ways. The most well-liked method is to trek up to the falls' summit. It takes roughly an hour to finish the approximately 3-kilometer (1.9-mile) hike. The views from the top of the falls are truly breathtaking, and the trek is rather simple. Taking a boat trip on the Diyaluma River is another method to see the Diyaluma Falls. You may get up close to the falls during the hour-long boat journey. The second tier of the falls is also visible from the boat trip. Any tourist to Sri Lanka must see the Diyaluma Falls since they are a genuinely breathtaking sight. You will undoubtedly be awed by the falls' magnificence whether you trek to the top of them, ride on a boat, or just observe them from a distance.

Additional information on Diyaluma Falls is provided below:

Sinhala for "two streams" is the name "Diyaluma".
British explorers made the initial discovery of the falls in the 19th century.
A well-liked location for whitewater rafting is the Diyaluma Falls.
Trekkers and hikers enjoy visiting the falls as well.
Diyaluma Falls are at their most picturesque during the monsoon season, which lasts from October to December.
