Nine Arches Bridge


Description

The Nine Arches Bridge, it‚Äôs a viaduct bridge in Sri Lanka. One of the nation's outstanding examples of railroad construction from the colonial era is this one. Between the Ella and Demodara train stations are where you'll find it in Demodara. Due to the inventive architecture of the bridge and the abundance of vegetation on the adjoining hillsides, tourism in the area has steadily increased.

The bridge's construction is typically credited to a local Ceylonese builder named P. K. Appuhami, who worked alongside British engineers. D J. Wimalasurendra, a renowned engineer and inventor from Ceylon, served as both the project's principal designer and project manager. Harold Cuthbert Marwood of the Railway Construction Department of the Ceylon Government Railway was the viaduct's designer. Details of all the records, including the designs and drawings, are included in the 1923 report "Construction of a Concrete Railway Viaduct in Ceylon" issued by the Engineering Association of Ceylon.

According to widespread rumors, when building on the bridge began, the Great War broke out between the European empires, and the steel originally earmarked for this site was instead diverted to Britain's war-related projects on the front lines. Due to the halt in work, the people had little choice but to construct the bridge out of stone bricks and cement rather than steel.
